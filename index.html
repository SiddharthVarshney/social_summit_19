reinvigorated, reimagined

“Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world” These powerful words from Nelson Mandela, in their very essence, highlight the importance and significance of Education in one’s life. Education, at its very core, gives an individual the knowledge to observe the world and change it, bit by bit, into something better. An educated mind is able to think intensively, critically and never accepts any idea at its face value. Education is the path towards enlightenment, the development of an aware mind. From time immemorial, India has been a hub of education for the World. Students from all over the world, came here to study at ancient educational institutes at Taxila and Nalanda. Gurukul system, with its origins in India, is considered to be one of the best approaches for childhood education focusing on the mutual development of teacher and child. Such is the significance of this system that its has decended into mythology and folklore. But, given the history of education in India, the current scenario has been charmless and the future of children studying in modern schools seems quite bleak. According to reports and surveys conducted by several International and National organizations, only 48.1% of children in Grade 5 can read texts from Grade 2 level books, almost 40% of children in Grade 3 still cannot read simple words and nearly 40% of children of children in Grade 4 cannot do subtraction. Despite the efforts by government and its various initiatives like Sarv Shiksha Abhiyan, 11.9 million children between the age of 6 and 13 are out of school. Very often, school classrooms are conducted in open areas. The situation of government schools has been super-critical. A lot has been said, announced but everything has been backburned and laid in abeyance. Now, “Its Time to Act”. With this inspiration in our minds, National Service Scheme, IIT Roorkee proudly presents National Social Summit 2019 under the theme “Empowerment through Education”, focusing on improving the education standards in India at the grass-root level. We look forward to being inspired by words of famous personalities, focusing on solving and finding solutions to existing issues, acknowledging the work done by various NGOs and Individuals to improve the standard of education, and bringing out various technical-cum-policy solutions through our series of events. We are looking at this Summit as tiny, yet significant, step towards the improvement of Education, and hope that it will bring out positive and fruitful results.

“The roots of education are bitter, but the fruit is sweet.”

-Aristotle
